Travelling in Tagawa

Photography Exhibition by Anne Virta

Tagawa (Tagawa-shi) city located in Fukuoka Prefecture in Kyushu Island, Japan. Tagawa is surrounded by mountains, Mount Hiko in the south. Hikosan Shrine, Fukuoka’s only Jingu shrine, the most prestigious Shinto shrine, nestled on sacred mountain Mount Hiko. Since ancient times people have visited the shrine to worship the god of safety in farming and mining.

In year 2009 “Peeks into the Dancing Moominvalley” (link to the Dancing Moominvalley blog) took part to a happening introducing Tove Jansson´s work and Finland in Fukuoka Asian Art Museum. I was photographing the working process and the show for children in Fukuoka Asian Art Museum.

I travelled with the dancers, and the producer and the composer of the music of the Dancing Moominvalley. We had an honour to stay in a Japanese family in Tagawa. It was awesome since to travelling to Japan has been my dream since I was twelve! So we climbed up the stairs to the Shinto Shrine in the holy Mount Hiko and I was touched by the the forest and the mountain with magnificent scenes and its ancient places and builings.

"Travelling in Tagawa"
Anne Virta
